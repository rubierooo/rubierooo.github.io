Rubie is an artist and songstress from south London. Their debut album Take Both is a manifesto for slipperiness - a soup-swimming experimental oddysey through gender & queer relationships. The album was assembled in their living room studio from home recordings, sampled mobile phone voice memos and public group singing exercises.

Centering their voice, Rubie’s writing starts with a-capella sketches, fleshed out through live experiments with an eclectic but minimal toolkit of sounds; A process flexible enough to produce the twitchy math-pop of Whole, to the blissful choral drones of Anatomy, or the crashy distorted experimental rock of The Need.



The often abstract approach to songwriting occasionally brings back memories of self-labelled ‘queer core’ project Sleeping States. No song does so more than closer Sailors.... There is a stunning moment near the end as Rubie sings “And I feel like I’m nearing the edge, I’ll cut my losses and jump” and the song drops suddenly to a swooning piano accompanied outro . It’s a captivating ending to a breathtaking and often thought-provoking debut album. - Beats to the Bar

The whole performance feels like a meditation on taking up space. Rather than shrinking, Rubie grows exponentially onstage... It is completely arresting to watch them perform, and even unassuming members of the public who have come to the Windmill for a Monday night pint seem caught up. - Freyegg for Brenda Magazine

Rubie vibrates during a set; bellowing with the might of an army and softly plunging like a cleavage - very soon you’re immersed in a total feeling - TheProphet4Profit
